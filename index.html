OpenXeroth is an open-source initiative that develops modular, AI-powered pipelines for real-time animal re-identification (re-ID) from continuous video streams, starting with livecams like Djuma Cam and extending to drone footage and underwater deployments. It bridges computer vision research and conservation impact by combining cutting-edge detection, tracking, and transformer-based re-ID models in a system designed for edge-to-local-to-cloud execution and live volunteer feedback via Slack. Unlike traditional photo-based platforms, OpenXeroth is purpose-built for real-time [video streaming] wildlife monitoring, enabling instant detection, species classification, and identity confirmation. Its architecture supports integration with established ecosystems like Wildbook, allowing OpenXeroth to focus on high-performance streaming pipelines while leveraging existing biodiversity databases and researcher tools. The project operates under the Apache 2.0 license, welcoming global contributors and field volunteers alike to collaboratively build the future of AI-driven animal re-identification.

What is Hosana?

Hosana is OpenXeroth’s first open-source project: a real-time wildlife re-identification pipeline. Built for 24/7 video streams, it leverages state-of-the-art detection and transformer-based re-ID to detect, track, classify and identify individual animals.

Pipeline Architecture

Hosana processes live video in real time using YOLOv8 detection, object tracking, species classification, and Vision Transformer-based re-identification, with a feedback loop via Slack.
